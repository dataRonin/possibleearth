Temperatures

Air Temperature and Dew Point

Air Temperature

The hottest temperature ever recorded on earth was 56.7 C in El Azizia, Libya, in 2012. The coldest temperature on earth was -89.2 C, at Vostok Station in Antarctica, in 1983.

Dew Point

Although controversy around the measurement exists, the highest dewpoint on earth ever recorded was in Dhahran, Saudi Arabia in 2003. At this time, the dry bulb temperature was 42.2 degrees. In Las Vegas, in 2007, a dewpoint of -22 C was recorded, which drew from temperatures above 45 C and relative humidities of less than 1 %.

Pressures

Atmospheric Pressure, Vapor Pressure Defecit, Vapor Pressure, Saturated Vapor Pressure

Atmospheric Pressure

The highest pressure recorded on earth was 1083.8 millibars, in Siberia, in 1968. The lowest pressure on earth was 870 millibars, set during a Typhoon Tip in 1979 in the Pacific Ocean.

Vapor Pressure Defecit

Vapor Pressure Defecit is a percentage, and can range between 0 and 100 percent. It is directly related to relative humidity, through air temperature.

Radiation

Net Solar Radiation and Photosynthetically Active Radiation

Net Solar Radiation

The earth can recieve a maximum of 1367 Watts/m2 of solar radiation. Realistically, depending on latitude and atmosphere, we receive about three-quarters of that. At night, we do not have direct incoming solar radiation (0 Watts/m2), although the earth is still experiencing convection at night.

Photosyntheically Active Radiation

Experiments have shown up to 1150 micromols per m2 per second photons as a maximum received. At night, 0 micromols per m2 per second photons are received.

Wind

Windspeed

Windspeed

A wind of 103.3 m/s was measured during Hurricane Olivia in 1996 just off the coast of Australia. The least possible wind is 0, since we can't have "negative wind".

Precipitation

Daily and Annual Precipitation

Daily Precipitation

1,825 mm of precipitation was measured in one day on the island of Reunion in the Indian Ocean in 1966 during a tropical storm.

Annual Precipitation

An Indian record from 1860 states that 26470 mm of rain were measured in 1861. However, the most recent record of high annual rainfall was 11872, also in India. For snow, 9900 mm of snow fell in Tamarack, CA in 1911. It accumulated atop existing snow to also set the deep snowpack record. In the dry valleys in Antarctica, there are places that have not had any precipitation for hundreds of years.

Relative Humidity

Relative Humidity (%)

Relative Humidity is a percentage, and ranges from 0 to 100. Relative humidity can get below 1 %, but this is very rare. Sensors may also detect relative humidities of greater than 100 %.
